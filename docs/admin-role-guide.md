# EduFlow Admin Role Guide

**Audience:** New admins being onboarded to EduFlow
**Platform:** EduFlow | Layaa AI
**Support:** Contact the school Owner for account setup

---

## What is the "Admin" role?

In EduFlow, **Admin** is not a single person — it is a family of six specialist roles. Each admin logs in with the same "Admin" account type, but the platform shows them only the tools that are relevant to their job.

Think of it like this: a school office has many departments. The accounts person doesn't need to manage bus routes, and the transport head doesn't need to print fee receipts. EduFlow reflects that reality.

The six Admin sub-roles are:

| Sub-Role | Who is this? |
|----------|-------------|
| **Principal** | The head of academics and day-to-day school operations |
| **Accountant** | The person handling fees, payments, and finance |
| **Receptionist** | Front-desk staff managing admissions and visitors |
| **Transport Head** | The person managing buses, routes, and student transport |
| **Maintenance** | The facilities team handling upkeep and repairs |
| **IT / Tech** | Technology support staff |

---

## How to Use This Guide

Each section below covers one sub-role. You will find:
- **What this person does** — a plain-English description of their job
- **Their tools** — every tool they can see in EduFlow, with a full explanation of what it does

## 1. Principal

### Who is the Principal?

The Principal is the most powerful admin. They oversee the entire school — academics, staff, discipline, communication, and facilities. They are the second-in-command after the Owner.

### Tools Available to the Principal

---

#### Principal Daily Dashboard
Your morning starting point. Every day when you log in, this screen shows:
- Which teachers are absent today
- Who has been assigned as a substitute
- Any pending approvals waiting for you
- Important alerts flagged from yesterday

Use this first thing every morning to know what needs your immediate attention.

---

#### Student Database
The complete list of every student enrolled in the school. You can:
- Search for any student by name or admission number
- View their full profile: class, section, guardian contact, date of birth
- See their current status (active / inactive)
- Add new students if needed

This is your go-to whenever a parent or teacher asks you about a specific student.

---

#### Attendance (Mark & Track)
Mark daily attendance for students. Select the class and date, then mark each student as Present, Absent, or Late, and submit.

> **Note:** The Principal can mark attendance for any class. Teachers mark attendance for their own assigned class only.

---

#### Attendance Overview
A visual report showing attendance trends over the past 3 months — across the entire school or for a specific class. Charts show overall attendance percentage month by month.

Use this to spot patterns: Is Class 7 consistently lower? Are Mondays worse than other days? This helps you take action before it becomes a serious problem.

---

#### Timetable Builder
Create and manage the weekly class schedule. For each class and section:
- Assign which teacher teaches which subject at which time
- View the full weekly grid
- Make changes when a teacher goes on leave or when the schedule changes

When you update the timetable here, teachers automatically see the change in their substitution viewer.

---

#### Certificates
Generate official school certificates for students:
- **Transfer Certificate (TC)** — Issued when a student leaves the school
- **Bonafide Certificate** — Confirms a student is currently enrolled
- **Character Certificate** — Official statement of student conduct

Select the student, choose the certificate type, and the system generates a formatted PDF ready for printing or email.

---

#### Circulars (Notices & Messages)
Send official notices and announcements to the school community. You can target:
- All staff
- All teachers
- All parents
- A specific class or section

Examples: exam schedule, holiday announcement, fee reminder, event notice.

---

#### Parent Messages
Send a personal message directly to the parent of a specific student. This is different from Circulars — it is one-to-one, not broadcast.

Use this for: absence follow-up, disciplinary communication, special requests.

---

#### Enquiry Register
When a parent visits or calls to ask about admission, their details are recorded here. Each enquiry has:
- Parent name and contact
- Child's age and current class
- Notes from the conversation

This is the starting point of the admissions process.

---

#### Admission Pipeline
Tracks every prospective student from first enquiry through to final admission. Stages typically include: Enquiry → Visit → Application → Docs Submitted → Admitted.

Use this to know how many students are in each stage and follow up with those who have gone quiet.

---

#### Staff Tracker
View profiles of every staff member: teachers, support staff, admin. See their:
- Contact details and qualifications
- Role and assigned classes
- Employment status

> The Principal can view staff records. The Owner can add, edit, or remove staff.

---

#### Staff Performance
Analytics on teacher and staff performance. Shows:
- Attendance percentage for each staff member
- Leave taken this month/year
- Any performance flags raised

Use this during appraisals or to spot a staff member who may need support.

---

#### Leave Manager (Approve / Reject)
When a teacher or staff member applies for leave, it appears here. You review the request and either approve or reject it.

> **Important:** Only the Principal and Owner can approve leave requests. Other admin sub-roles cannot.

After your decision, the staff member is notified automatically.

---

#### Incidents & Visitors
A log with two purposes:
1. **Student incidents** — Record disciplinary events: fights, misconduct, repeated rule violations. Each entry has the student name, date, description, and action taken.
2. **Visitor entries** — Log who visited the school, when, and for what purpose.

This provides a paper trail for disciplinary decisions and school security.

---

#### Smart Alerts
The platform watches for exceptions and flags them here automatically. Examples:
- A class with attendance below 75%
- A student absent for more than 5 consecutive days
- A fee account with large outstanding dues

You don't need to check reports manually — the system comes to you when something needs attention.

---

#### Transport Manager
View and manage student transport:
- See which students are assigned to which bus route
- View route details and pickup/drop times
- Assign or reassign students to routes

> The Transport Head is the day-to-day manager of transport. The Principal uses this for oversight.

---

#### School Activities
Track extracurricular life of the school:
- **House points** — Record points for each school house (academic, sports, cultural)
- **Sports & competitions** — Log results and achievements
- **Awards and recognitions** — Record certificates and titles won

This feeds into end-of-year awards and report cards.

---

#### Doc Scanner
Upload a physical document (photo or scanned PDF) and the system extracts the text and files it against the relevant student or staff record.

Useful for filing admission forms, parent-signed consent letters, and certificates submitted at registration.

---

#### Facility Requests (Maintenance Queue)
View all pending maintenance requests raised by teachers, students, and other staff. You can:
- See what is open, in-progress, or resolved
- Prioritize urgent items
- Assign tasks to the maintenance team

> The Principal approves and oversees. The Maintenance sub-role is the one actually doing and logging the work.

---

#### Report an Issue
Raise a new maintenance request yourself. If you spot a broken chair, a leaking pipe, or a broken projector while walking through school, log it here directly.

---

#### Audit Log
A complete record of every action taken on the platform by every user. Who added a student, who approved a leave, who edited a fee record — everything is recorded with the user name, timestamp, and details.

Use this to investigate a dispute, verify an action was taken, or check accountability.

---

#### Form Builder
Create custom forms and surveys that can be sent to parents, students, or staff. Examples: feedback surveys, event registration forms, permission slips.

---

#### Query & Support
Submit a support ticket if something is not working correctly or you need help from the platform operator.

## 2. Accountant

### Who is the Accountant?

The Accountant handles all money-related tasks: recording fee payments, tracking who has paid and who hasn't, generating receipts, and following up with defaulters.

### Tools Available to the Accountant

---

#### Student Database
A read-only view of all students, used for looking up which student a payment belongs to before recording it.

---

#### Fee Tracker
The Accountant's main workspace. This tool lets you:
- **Record a payment** — Select the student, enter the amount, choose the payment mode (cash / online / cheque), and save. A receipt is generated automatically.
- **View payment history** — See all transactions filtered by class, payment status, or date range.
- **Class-wise summary** — See how much has been collected vs. how much is outstanding for each class.

---

#### Fee Defaulters
A list of students who have not paid fees on time. For each defaulter, you can:
- See the outstanding amount and how overdue it is
- Send an SMS or WhatsApp reminder to the parent directly from this screen

This saves you from making individual phone calls — the platform handles the reminders.

---

#### Fee Receipts
Generate and download PDF receipts for any fee transaction. You can:
- Search by student name or transaction ID
- Download a single receipt or export a batch as a spreadsheet
- Re-print a receipt if a parent asks for a duplicate

---

#### Form Builder
Create financial data collection forms — for example, a bank details form for parents who want to switch to online payment.

---

#### Query & Support
Submit a support ticket if you encounter a technical issue.

---

### Key Accountant Connections

- **With Principal:** If a student has a fee dispute or discount that requires approval, the Accountant flags it and the Principal (or Owner) approves.
- **With Receptionist:** New admissions come from the Receptionist. Once a student is admitted, the Accountant sets up their fee account.

## 3. Receptionist

### Who is the Receptionist?

The Receptionist is the school's front desk. They manage the first impression — handling enquiries, processing admissions, managing visitors, and taking care of paperwork for students joining or leaving.

### Tools Available to the Receptionist

---

#### Student Database
Look up any student quickly at the front desk. Useful when a parent comes in asking about their child.

---

#### Enquiry Register
When someone calls or walks in asking about admission, log their details here:
- Parent's name and phone number
- Child's name, age, and current school
- Any notes from the conversation

This is step one of the admissions process.

---

#### Admission Pipeline
Track every prospective student through the admissions stages. Move each enquiry along as they progress: visited school, submitted application, submitted documents, payment received, admitted.

This gives a clear picture of how many students are close to joining and which ones need a follow-up call.

---

#### Parent Messages
Send a message to a specific parent — for example, to ask them to bring a document, confirm an admission appointment, or share joining instructions.

---

#### Student Transfer (Withdrawal & TC)
When a student is leaving the school, process their transfer here:
- Mark the student as withdrawn
- Generate the Transfer Certificate (TC)
- Record the reason for leaving

---

#### ID Cards (Generate & Print)
Generate the official school ID card for a student. Select the student, review the details, and print. Used during new admissions and when a student loses their card.

---

#### Asset Tracker
Keep an inventory of school assets — furniture, equipment, books, uniforms. Log new items, track their condition, and note if something has been issued or returned.

---

#### Incidents & Visitors
Log all visitor entries:
- Who visited (name, contact, purpose)
- Check-in and check-out time
- Who they met

This is the school's visitor register, now digital.

---

#### Report an Issue
If you notice something broken at the front desk or in the reception area, log a maintenance request here.

---

#### Form Builder
Create admission forms, parent information forms, and feedback surveys.

---

#### Query & Support
Submit a support ticket if you encounter an issue.

---

### Key Receptionist Connections

- **With Principal:** New students admitted by the Receptionist show up in the Principal's student list immediately.
- **With Accountant:** Once a student is admitted, the Accountant uses their profile to record admission fees.
- **With Maintenance:** Issues spotted at the front desk are logged via Report an Issue and seen by the Maintenance team.

## 4. Transport Head

### Who is the Transport Head?

The Transport Head manages all school transport — buses, routes, and which students travel by school bus. They make sure pickups and drop-offs run smoothly and efficiently.

### Tools Available to the Transport Head

---

#### Student Database
View the full student list to identify which students are enrolled in transport and need route assignments.

---

#### Transport Manager
The main workspace for managing school transport:
- **Routes** — Create and name routes (e.g., "Route A – Civil Lines"), set stop sequence, and assign a bus
- **Buses** — Log each bus with registration number, driver name, and capacity
- **Student assignments** — Assign each transport student to a route and stop
- **View roster** — See all students on each route with their pickup points

---

#### Route Optimisation
An AI-powered analysis tool that looks at where transport students live (using location data) and suggests the most efficient groupings and routes.

Use this when:
- You have new students joining transport
- You want to reduce travel time or fuel cost
- You are redesigning routes for a new academic year

---

#### Asset Tracker
Track bus fleet details and equipment:
- Vehicle registration and insurance expiry
- Fuel records and service history
- Any equipment (GPS devices, first aid kits) assigned to each bus

---

#### Form Builder
Create transport request forms — for example, a form for parents to apply for the school bus service.

---

#### Query & Support
Submit a support ticket if you encounter an issue.

---

### Key Transport Head Connections

- **With Principal:** The Principal has view access to Transport Manager for oversight. The Transport Head is the day-to-day manager.
- **With Receptionist:** When a new student is admitted who needs transport, the Receptionist marks this during admission and the Transport Head assigns the route.

## 5. Maintenance

### Who is the Maintenance team?

The Maintenance sub-role handles school upkeep — plumbing, electrical, cleaning, furniture repairs, and all physical facility work. They receive tasks from the school and manage their completion.

### Tools Available to Maintenance

---

#### Maintenance Schedule
A calendar of preventive maintenance tasks — things that need to happen regularly regardless of whether something has broken:
- Monthly fire extinguisher checks
- Quarterly generator servicing
- Annual painting and deep cleaning

Add tasks with a frequency (weekly, monthly, yearly) and due date. The system reminds you when something is coming up.

---

#### Vendor Log
A directory of all contractors and service providers:
- Electrician, plumber, pest control, AC servicing, etc.
- Their contact details, last service date, and contract details

When something breaks, you know exactly who to call. You also have a record of every vendor who has worked on school premises.

---

#### Query & Support
Submit a support ticket if you encounter a platform issue.

---

### Key Maintenance Connections

- **Receives requests from everyone:** Teachers, receptionists, transport staff, and even the Principal can raise maintenance requests. All requests land in the Facility Requests queue (visible to Owner and Principal). The Maintenance team picks these up and resolves them.
- **Reports back to Principal/Owner:** Once a task is resolved, it is marked complete. The Principal can see the full queue and resolution status.

## 6. IT / Tech

### Who is the IT / Tech admin?

The IT admin handles technology support for the school — computers, printers, internet, projectors, and other tech equipment.

### Tools Available to IT / Tech

---

#### Tech Issues
An IT help desk tracker. Anyone in the school can raise a tech support request, and it appears here. You can:
- View all open tech tickets
- Pick up a ticket and mark it "In Progress"
- Add notes on what you found and what you did
- Mark it as resolved once fixed

This creates a history of every tech issue in the school — useful for tracking recurring problems or demonstrating work done.

---

#### Report an Issue
Raise a maintenance (non-tech) request. For example, if a server room has a leaking pipe, the IT person can log it for the Maintenance team.

---

#### Form Builder
Create forms for tech-related requests — for example, a "Request a new device" form or a "Report a software issue" form.

---

#### Query & Support
Submit a platform support ticket.

---

### Key IT / Tech Connections

- **Receives requests from all roles:** Any staff member can submit a tech issue, which lands in the IT admin's Tech Issues queue.
- **Reports to Owner/Principal:** The Owner and Principal can see the full tech issues log for oversight.

### The Approval Chain

Some actions require a specific sub-role to approve:

| Action | Who initiates | Who approves |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| Staff leave request | Teacher / any staff | **Principal or Owner only** |
| Fee discount or waiver | Accountant flags it | **Owner only** |
| Maintenance priority | Any staff raises request | **Principal or Owner reviews** |
| Student TC (transfer) | **Receptionist** generates | No separate approval needed |
| Leave approval for a teacher | Teacher submits | **Principal or Owner** |

## Common Workflows Step by Step

### New Student Admission

1. Parent enquires → **Receptionist** logs in Enquiry Register
2. Parent visits and decides to join → **Receptionist** moves to Admission Pipeline
3. Documents submitted, form filled → **Receptionist** processes admission in Student Database
4. Student appears in system → **Accountant** records admission fee and creates fee account
5. Student needs transport → **Receptionist** marks transport required; **Transport Head** assigns route
6. **Principal** can see the new student in Student Database immediately

---

### Staff Leave Request

1. Teacher submits leave application from their account
2. Request appears in **Leave Manager** for the **Principal**
3. Principal reviews and approves or rejects
4. Teacher gets a notification of the decision
5. If approved, **Principal** checks Timetable Builder to assign a substitute

---

### Fee Collection Day

1. Parent comes to pay fees
2. **Accountant** opens Fee Tracker → selects class → selects student → enters amount and payment mode → saves
3. Receipt is generated automatically
4. If parent asks for a printed receipt, **Accountant** goes to Fee Receipts and prints it
5. If parent hasn't paid and is overdue, **Accountant** finds them in Fee Defaulters and sends an SMS reminder

---

### Maintenance Request

1. A teacher notices a broken projector and raises it via **Report an Issue**
2. The request appears in **Facility Requests** (visible to Principal and Owner)
3. **Maintenance** team opens Maintenance Schedule, picks up the task
4. After fixing it, they mark it resolved
5. **Principal** sees it resolved in the Facility Requests queue

## Tips for New Admins

**You will only see tools relevant to your sub-role.** Don't worry if you see fewer menu items than someone else — that's by design.

**The AI Chat is your shortcut.** Instead of navigating to a tool, you can type naturally in the chat panel:
- "How many students are absent today?" (Principal)
- "Record ₹5,000 payment from Arjun Sharma of Class 8" (Accountant)
- "Show all open tech tickets" (IT/Tech)
- "Who visited school today?" (Receptionist)

The AI will ask for confirmation before making any changes, so you won't accidentally record something wrong.

**Everything is logged.** Every action you take is recorded in the Audit Log. If you make a mistake, contact the Principal or Owner — they can look up exactly what happened and arrange a correction.

**You cannot see data from other schools.** EduFlow is used by multiple schools, but your account is locked to your school's data only.
